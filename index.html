Employment history

For the first five years of my career I worked at the Cornwall Wildlife Trust, starting as a Receptionist and working my way up to a Human Resources Assistant. I learnt many skills during my time at the Trust but after five enjoyable years I felt it was time to broaden my horizons and experience something different. Since leaving the Trust I have volunteered in The Philippines with International Citizens Service, backpacked independently around Europe, worked in Ibiza, gained retail and customer service experience at Clark’s shoes along with a seasonal catering position at my local bakery. When returning from my travels I worked for King’s Service Centre as a Switchboard Analyst which was a fantastic opportunity and gave me the chance to acquire a knowledge of the education sector as well as IT support, this has led to my current role as a SITs Apprentice with King's Service Centre.

Social life

In my spare time I enjoy volunteering for various charities, mainly Samaritans and The Cornwall Blind Association. I have been recognised for my efforts with my biggest achievments including carrying the Olympic Torch in 2012 and winning the Local Hero's Award for 'Inspiration to Young People'. Along with my charity work I love to travel and I am heavily involved in promotion for a monthly music event through managing the street team for the promotions.
